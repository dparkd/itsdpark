During my internship at This Also, I was tasked in exploring how college students can stay healthier using technology with data visualizations.

Problem

A lot of the health applications today share the same visual language when representing health - Graphs and charts are good for understanding data, but it isn't inspirational.

How might we represent health data in a unique way so that students can become more aware of their health?

Solution

Reshape is a mobile application that generates unique shapes using daily health statistics (distance walked, hours asleep, steps taken, etc.). From the shapes generated, the user can interact with their health by drawing their 'daily journal' and viewing their breakdowns.

By representing health in a new way through data visualization and interactivity, the hope is to bring more awareness to students of their health.

Internship Reflection: "Brute Force Your Designs"

One of the directors (Brian) encouraged me to generate as many crazy ideas isolated from one another when I was stuck in the design process. This method helped me breakthrough and think about unique and new interactions.
